stones for the rampart

Aleksander Kamiński

Description of books

The plot of the book is based on real events from the end of the 1930s and the period of the German occupation in Warsaw. After the capital is occupied by the Germans, the three main characters try to find themselves in the new reality. In October 1939, they joined the left-wing Polish People's Independence Action (PLAN), taking part in its actions of small sabotage, incl. they stick notes on the announcement of Governor Hans Frank about the creation of the General Government, on which they read: Marshal Piłsudski would say: and we all have you. They also undertake various types of paid work.

They left PLAN a month before the organization was broken up by the Germans, looking for new opportunities to act in the underground. At the same time they learn, incl. organize discussion meetings. In March 1941, they joined the Gray Ranks of the Warsaw Banner and began their activity in the "Wawer" Small Sabotage Organization. In November 1942 they become members of the Assault Groups.

After Janek Bytnar "Rudy" was arrested by the Gestapo, "Zośka" organizes the rescue of his friend on March 26, 1943. "Rudy" was released, but as a result of injuries sustained during torture by Gestapo officers, he died on March 30, 1943. He died mortally wounded on the same day during the action "Alek". "Zośka" dies in action in Sieczychy on August 20, 1943.

About the author

Aleksander Kamiński

Aleksander Kamiński lived in the years 1903-1978. He was a devoted pedagogue, scout instructor, scoutmaster, one of the soldiers of the Home Army and the leader of the Gray Ranks. He was nicknamed "pebble". In 1943-44 he wrote the book "Stones for the Rampart". The title was an initiative of one of its main characters - Zośka, it refers to Rudy's favorite poem by Juliusz Słowacki.
